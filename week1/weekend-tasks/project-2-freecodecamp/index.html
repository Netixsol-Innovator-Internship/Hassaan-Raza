Tribute to Maulana Bashir Farooqui

Maulana Bashir Farooqui is a religious scholar and philanthropist who has dedicated his life to serving humanity through the Saylani Welfare International Trust. His voluntary leadership has enabled large-scale charitable initiatives impacting millions every month.

Early Life & Education

Bashir Ahmed Polani was born on March 3, 1963, during the month of Ramazan in Shikarpur, Sindh, into a noble and religious family of the Bantawa Community. His father, Abdul Ghaffar Polani, was a successful businessman.

He completed his schooling at Islamia High School Shikarpur and in 1975 moved to Karachi to pursue college and Islamic studies. He enrolled at Abdullah Haroon College for his intermediate education, and afterward studied Dars-e-Nizami at Darul-ul-Uloom Amjadia.

Saylani Welfare

In the early 1990s, Maulana Bashir Farooqi, along with others, began charitable and social work. On May 15, 1999, he formally established the Saylani Welfare International Trust. Since its founding, he has led the organization as its voluntary chairman, steering its projects and mission without financial gain.

Impact

Saylani Welfare Trust under his leadership impacts millions of lives every month through sustained large-scale efforts:

  • Spending Rs. 10 Million each month on various welfare projects
  • Teaching 20,000 students monthly
  • Treating 50,000 patients every month
  • Feeding 21 million people each month
